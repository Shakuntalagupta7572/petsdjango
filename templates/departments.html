Hepatology

The study of liver, gallbladder, biliary tree, and pancreas diseases, including prevention, diagnosis, and management

Disorders of the liver are common in dogs and cats. A wide range of clinical signs can be seen such as jaundice, hepatic encephalopathy, gastrointestinal signs like decreased appetite, weight loss, vomiting or diarrhoea, polyuria, polydipsia, ascites or coagulopathy.

Neurology

The treatment and study of conditions that affect the brain, spinal cord, nerves and muscles

Veterinary Neurology is the branch of medicine that treats diseases of the nervous system: the brain, spinal cord, nerves, and muscles in pets. This encompasses such common problems as epilepsy, herniated disks, spinal and head injuries, meningitis, and cancers of the nervous system

skin care

Skin Care department that can perform allergy testing and create a treatment plan for a pet's skin issues

Skin care for pets is an important part of their overall health and wellbeing. The skin is the largest organ of the body and is the first line of defense against bacteria and infection. A pet's skin is also the first thing visitors to your home will notice. Therefore, it is important to provide proper skin care for your pet.

Eye care

Avoid using irritating soaps or sprays near your pet's eyes

A veterinarian is usually able to diagnose and treat most common eye conditions in pets. some cases may require a referral to a veterinary ophthalmologist for further testing, treatment, or surgery. A veterinary ophthalmologist is a veterinarian who specializes in treating eye disorders in animals.
